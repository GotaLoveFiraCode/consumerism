Throw-away Culture

Throwaway culture is a harmful trend that has emerged around the late 90s. It promotes the concept of “single-use clothing” made out of cheap material with minimal manufacturing costs. Throwaway culture is extremely harmful for the workers making the clothes, as each time a brand wants to lower its costs, it comes at an expense to the working conditions, salaries, and safety precautions in factories. It has also worsened air pollution and textile waste by a large margin, due to the excess production of materials.

How to Recognize Quality in Garments?

How do you know if something is high quality? What does that even mean?

Stitching

Stitching is all about two things: cost and time.

  • Stitch-Length/Stitches-per-Inch: 0.5 to 3.0 mmstitches are good
  • Stitches should be straight, tight, and lie flat
  • Look for a matching or pleasingly contrasting color
  • Test: can you see through the stitching when pulling the fabric apart?
  • Loose threads are a very bad sign...
  • Look out for sergers! They’re popular, but not very robust.
